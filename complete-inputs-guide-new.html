₹0 — own farm
NPK role
N fixation (8–10 kg/dose), micronutrient release, soil biology
Apply
200 L/acre via irrigation channel — 3 doses at Day 0, 20, 45
Ingredients per 200 L drum
10 kg fresh cow dung5–10 L cow urine2 kg jaggery2 kg any flour1 handful forest soil200 L water
Preparation — 7 days
1
Add all ingredients into drum; stir vigorously clockwise for 10 minutes
2
Cover with gunny cloth (not airtight); keep in shade
3
Stir daily for 7 days; smell should turn pleasantly sour-earthy
4
Strain through cloth; pour into irrigation channel when field is flooding
5
Apply in evening — microbes are heat-sensitive; do not apply in afternoon sun

Panchagavyam
₹0 — own farm
NPK role
Growth hormones, amino acids, vitamins — boosts N uptake efficiency by 25–30%
Apply
3% foliar spray, 25 L/acre — at tillering, panicle init, booting stages
Ingredients for 10 L batch
1 kg cow dung1 L cow urine1 L cow milk250 ml curd250 ml ghee3 ripe bananas500 ml coconut water500 ml sugarcane juice
Preparation — 30 days
1
Combine all ingredients in a clay or plastic pot; mash bananas well
2
Cover loosely with cloth; keep in shade, stir twice daily
3
Ferment for 30 days; colour turns dark brown, smell turns sweet-sour
4
Strain well; dilute 30 ml per 1 L water (3%) before spraying
5
Spray early morning or after 5 PM; never in midday heat
High-yield tip
Spray at 50% flowering specifically — this single application fills hollow/empty grains and can increase grain weight by 8–12%

Wood Ash / Paddy Husk Ash
₹0 — own farm
Nutrients
High Potassium (5–7%) + Calcium + Silica — all three critical for lodging resistance
Apply
50–100 kg/acre broadcast; or dissolve in water and spray at booting
Why it matters for high yield
Silica from ash hardens stem and leaf — prevents lodging (falling over) when grain head becomes heavy. Paddy that lodges loses 20–30% yield. Ash also raises soil pH gradually if your soil is acidic.
How to use
1
Collect dry wood ash or paddy husk ash after burning
2
Broadcast 50 kg/acre in standing water at tillering; trample in
3
Or dissolve 1 kg ash in 10 L water, strain, spray on leaves at booting

Zinc Sulphate (organic-approved) / Zinc from Cow Sources
Very low cost
Nutrients
Zinc — deficiency is #1 hidden yield-loss cause in Tamil Nadu paddy soils
Apply
0.5% foliar spray (5g per litre) at tillering; or 5 kg/acre at basal
Signs of zinc deficiency to watch for
Rusty-brown spots on lower leaves at 2–3 weeks after transplant. Leaves turn pale green with reddish veins. Growth stalls. Very common in Villupuram wetland soils.
Free alternative
Amirtha Karaisal and Meen Amilam both supply zinc organically. If you apply both consistently, separate zinc supplement may not be needed — watch your crop for deficiency signs first before buying.

Azospirillum
₹60–80 per kg, buy once
Function
Fixes atmospheric N; produces growth hormones (IAA) that increase root volume by 30%
Apply
1 kg/acre mixed with 50 kg compost; apply in soil at basal. OR seed treatment 600 g/kg seed
How to apply
1
Mix 1 kg Azospirillum packet with 50 kg dry compost in shade
2
Spread evenly in field just before transplanting
3
Do not mix with chemical fertilizer or fungicide — kills the culture
4
Store in cool shade — do not expose to sunlight
Key rule
Never apply Azospirillum on same day as any chemical. Wait 3 days minimum if transitioning from chemical farm.
Basal (soil)Seed treatment
Phosphobacteria (PSB)
₹60–80 per kg, buy once
Function
Solubilises locked P in soil — unlocks 10–15 kg P/acre already present but unavailable to roots
Apply
1 kg/acre mixed with compost at basal; apply together with Azospirillum
Why this matters for wetland
Villupuram wetland soils often have adequate total P but most of it is locked as calcium phosphate or iron phosphate. PSB bacteria produce organic acids that dissolve these — no buying phosphorus fertilizer needed.
Basal (soil)
Trichoderma viride
₹80 per kg, buy once

Biological fungicide — colonises roots, prevents sheath blight, blast, damping-off in nursery
Apply
4 g/kg seed at nursery; 1 kg/acre mixed in compost at basal; soil drench at transplanting
Three-point application
1
Seed: dust 4g per kg on damp seeds before nursery sowing
2
Soil: mix 1 kg in 50 kg compost; apply in field at basal
3
Root dip: mix 5g per litre water; dip seedling roots for 30 min before transplanting

Pseudomonas fluorescens
₹80 per kg, buy once
Function
Produces antibiotics that suppress blast and bacterial leaf blight — major yield threats in Tamil Nadu
Apply
2.5 kg/acre as foliar spray at tillering and booting — dissolve in water 1:250
Why it matters
Rice blast alone can destroy 30–50% of yield in a wet season. Pseudomonas is the most effective bio-control against blast — Tamil Nadu Agriculture University recommends it specifically for this region.
